Tuscan Butter Mushrooms

BY ALFRED WATSON | DECEMBER 2018

TRENDING:

Tuscan Butter Mushrooms are a heavenly delicious side dish of tender baby Portabella mushrooms simmered slow in a creamy garlic butter sauce punctuated with bright tomatoes, wilted spinach and a sprinkle of bacon. We are talking nothing less than roll-back-the-clouds good when spooned over a juicy steak, grilled chops, salmon or even atop pasta or potatoes. INGREDIENTS REQUIRED:

  • 4 tbsp. butter
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp. tomato paste
  • 1 lb. baby bella mushrooms, cleaned
  • 1 c. cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 3/4 c. heavy cream
  • 1/4 c. freshly grated Parmesan
  • Kosher salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Pinch crushed red pepper flakes
  • 3 c. spinach
  • Thinly sliced basil, for garnish

Video receipe of Tuscan Butter Mushrooms:

PROCESS:

1.In a large skillet over medium heat, melt butter.
Add garlic and tomato paste andcook until fragrant, 1 minute.

2. Add mushrooms and tomatoes and cook until mushrooms
are tender and tomatoes are starting to burst,
5 minutes.

3.Add heavy cream and Parmesan and season with salt,
pepper,and a pinch of red pepper flakes, then bring to a
simmer.

4. Add spinach and cook until sauce is thickened
and spinach iswilted, 5 minutes.

5.Garnish with basil before serving.
